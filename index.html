About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Cleveland State University specializing in advanced manufacturing and materials processing. My research focuses on microstructure–property relationships in high-temperature alloys, particularly graphene and carbon nanotube reinforced Inconel 718 composites processed via Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) and Selective Laser Melting (SLM).

My work integrates powder metallurgy, additive manufacturing, thermomechanical processing, and advanced characterization techniques to design high-performance structural materials for demanding environments.

Research Focus Areas

  • Additive Manufacturing (SLM, Binder Jetting)
  • Powder Metallurgy (Spark Plasma Sintering, HIP)
  • Graphene & CNT Reinforced Metal Matrix Composites
  • Tribology and High-Temperature Oxidation
  • SEM/EDS, XRD, EBSD, FIB-TEM Characterization
